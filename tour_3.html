Literary Alcalá

The cultural importance of the city, its great university tradition, has enabled its history is full of writers who described the thought or were born in it. This signaling develops a journey through places related to the most important writers that relate to Alcalá. The relationship of writers and plaques that appear are:


  • Juan Ruiz Hita - Palace Square
  • San Ignacio de Loyola -Hospital Antezana
  • Santa Teresa de Jesus - Calle de la Imagen
  • John of the Cross - St. Thomas Aquinas Street
  • Miguel de Cervantes - Street Image
  • Fray Luis de Leon - street schools
  • Alonso Fernández de Avellaneda - Plaza Puerta de Madrid
  • Matthew German -Calle Pedro Gumiel
  • Francisco de Quevedo - street Bookshelves
  • Lope Felix de Vega Carpio - College Street
  • Pedro Calderon de la Barca - street Bookshelves
  • Gaspar Melchor de Jovellanos - Street Pedro Gumiel
  • Maria Isidra Quintina de Guzman - street Bookshelves
  • Leandro Fernandez de Moratin - street of Hue
  • Miguel de Unamuno - Plaza del Padre Lecande
  • Manuel Azana - Calle Image
  • Camilo Jose Cela - Renfe Station


STARTING POINT:

We started in the old hostelry of Students, XVI-XVII centuries, next to the Municipal Tourist Office Plaza de Cervantes.

DURATION:

Duration: 2.30 hours approx.

PRICE:

10.00 € , children under 5 free.

HOURS AND DAYS:
